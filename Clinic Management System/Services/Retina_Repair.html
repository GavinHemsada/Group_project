Retina Repair

What is Retina Repair surgery?

Retina Repair surgery at Eagle Vision Eye Care Clinic is a specialized procedure designed to treat a range of retinal conditions that can cause vision loss or impairment. The retina, located at the back of the eye, plays a crucial role in vision by converting light into signals sent to the brain. Damage to the retina from conditions like retinal detachment, tears, or macular degeneration can lead to severe vision issues. Retina Repair surgery aims to restore or preserve vision by addressing these issues through advanced surgical techniques.

Our highly skilled team, with over a decade of experience, performs procedures like vitrectomy, laser photocoagulation, or scleral buckling, depending on the specific condition. Using state-of-the-art equipment, we ensure precise, effective treatment tailored to each patient’s needs. Our goal is not only to repair the retina but to improve the quality of life by protecting your vision for the long term.

What conditions can be treated with Retina Repair at Eagle Vision Eye Care Clinic?

At Eagle Vision Eye Care Clinic, Retina Repair can treat a variety of retinal conditions, including:
  • Retinal tears or detachment
  • Diabetic retinopathy
  • Age-related macular degeneration (AMD)
  • Macular holes
  • Retinal vein occlusion

These conditions, if left untreated, can lead to vision loss, but with timely intervention, our expert team can often restore or preserve vision through specialized Retina Repair surgeries.

How is Retina Repair surgery performed at Eagle Vision Eye Care Clinic?

Retina Repair surgery at Eagle Vision is performed using advanced techniques and tools that are tailored to the specific retinal condition. Common procedures include:
  • Vitrectomy: A surgery where the vitreous gel inside the eye is removed to repair the retina, often used for retinal detachment or macular holes.
  • Laser Photocoagulation: A laser is used to seal retinal tears or stop abnormal blood vessels from leaking, commonly used for conditions like diabetic retinopathy.
  • Tonometry: Measures the pressure inside your eye to check for glaucoma.
  • Scleral Buckling: A small band is placed around the eye to gently push the retina back into place, typically used to treat retinal detachment.

    • Each procedure is performed by our experienced surgeons using cutting-edge technology, ensuring the highest precision and best possible outcomes for your vision.

What are the symptoms that may indicate the need for Retina Repair surgery?

Symptoms that may suggest retinal damage and the need for Retina Repair surgery include:
  • Sudden appearance of floaters (small dark spots or shapes in your vision)
  • Flashes of light in one or both eyes
  • Blurred or distorted vision
  • A shadow or curtain covering part of your vision
  • Sudden and significant loss of vision

    • If you experience any of these symptoms, it’s important to seek immediate medical attention, as conditions like retinal detachment can lead to permanent vision loss if not treated promptly. At Eagle Vision Eye Care Clinic, our expert team will conduct a thorough eye exam to determine if Retina Repair is necessary.

Are there any risks associated with Retina Repair surgery?

Like any surgical procedure, Retina Repair comes with potential risks, but these are minimized when performed by experienced specialists. Common risks include:
  • Infection
  • Increased eye pressure (glaucoma)
  • Cataract development (especially after vitrectomy)
  • Bleeding inside the eye
  • Retinal re-detachment

    • At Eagle Vision Eye Care Clinic, our surgeons use state-of-the-art equipment and techniques to reduce these risks. We take every precaution to ensure a safe and successful outcome, providing ongoing follow-up care to address any complications promptly.
