Laser Surgery

What is laser eye surgery?

Laser eye surgery is a medical procedure used to correct vision problems, such as nearsightedness (myopia), farsightedness (hyperopia), and astigmatism, by reshaping the cornea, the clear front part of the eye. The most common type of laser eye surgery is LASIK (Laser-Assisted in Situ Keratomileusis), where a laser is used to create a small flap in the cornea, allowing the underlying tissue to be reshaped. This adjusts how light is focused on the retina, improving vision. Another option is PRK (Photorefractive Keratectomy), where the outer layer of the cornea is removed and the tissue beneath is reshaped. Laser eye surgery is a popular choice because it can significantly reduce or eliminate the need for glasses or contact lenses, and recovery is typically fast, with most patients experiencing improved vision within a day or two.

What vision problems can laser surgery correct?

Laser surgery is primarily used to correct refractive errors, which affect how light is focused in the eye. These include:
  • Nearsightedness (myopia): When the cornea is too curved, light focuses in front of the retina, making distant objects appear blurry.
  • Farsightedness (hyperopia): When the cornea is too flat, light focuses behind the retina, making close objects appear blurry.
  • Astigmatism: When the cornea is irregularly shaped, light focuses unevenly, causing blurred or distorted vision.

How does laser eye surgery work?

During laser eye surgery, a specialized laser is used to remove a small amount of corneal tissue, reshaping the cornea to correct the way light is focused onto the retina. In LASIK, a thin flap is created in the cornea, lifted, and the laser reshapes the underlying tissue before the flap is replaced. In PRK, the outer layer of the cornea is removed entirely, and the laser reshapes the corneal tissue beneath. Both procedures aim to improve vision and reduce the need for glasses or contact lenses.

Who is a good candidate for laser eye surgery?

Ideal candidates for laser eye surgery are adults over the age of 18 who have had stable vision for at least one year. Other factors include:
  • Good overall eye health: You should not have any active eye diseases such as glaucoma, cataracts, or severe dry eye.
  • Adequate corneal thickness: For LASIK, the cornea must be thick enough to allow safe reshaping.
  • Realistic expectations: Candidates should understand that while laser surgery can greatly reduce dependence on glasses or contact lenses, it may not always achieve perfect vision, and there may be a need for touch-ups or future corrections.

What are the benefits of laser eye surgery?

The main benefits of laser eye surgery include:
  • Improved vision: Most patients achieve 20/20 vision or better after the procedure.
  • Reduced dependence on corrective lenses: Many people no longer need glasses or contact lenses after surgery, or they require them only for specific tasks like reading.
  • Quick recovery: Recovery is usually fast, with many patients noticing improved vision within 24 hours and full healing in a few weeks.

What are the risks or side effects of laser surgery?

While laser eye surgery is generally safe, there are some risks and side effects, which can include:
  • Dry eyes: Temporary dry eye symptoms are common after surgery and may require artificial tears or other treatments.
  • Glare and halos: Some patients may experience glare or halos around lights, particularly at night.
  • Under-correction or over-correction: In some cases, the surgery may not fully correct vision or may over-correct, requiring further treatment or enhancements.
  • Flap complications (for LASIK): In rare cases, issues can occur with the healing of the corneal flap.

How can you prepare for laser eye surgery?

To prepare for laser eye surgery, your doctor will conduct a thorough eye exam to assess your suitability for the procedure. This may include measuring the thickness of your cornea, checking for refractive errors, and evaluating your overall eye health. Prior to surgery:
  • Stop wearing contact lenses for a few weeks before the exam, as they can change the shape of your cornea.
  • Avoid eye makeup, creams, or lotions on the day of surgery to reduce the risk of infection.

What is the recovery process like after laser eye surgery?

After laser eye surgery, most patients experience improved vision within 24 to 48 hours. However, full recovery and stabilization of vision may take a few weeks to a few months. During recovery:
  • Avoid rubbing your eyes: This can interfere with the healing process, particularly after LASIK, where a flap is created.
  • Use prescribed eye drops: These help prevent infection, reduce inflammation, and manage dry eye symptoms.
  • Limit screen time and exposure to bright light: Protect your eyes from strain by taking frequent breaks and wearing sunglasses in bright conditions.
  • Follow-up appointments: Attend all post-surgery checkups to ensure proper healing and address any concerns.

Is laser eye surgery a permanent solution?

Laser eye surgery provides long-lasting vision correction, but it may not always be permanent. Over time, natural changes in the eye, such as age-related presbyopia (difficulty focusing on near objects), can affect your vision. In some cases, additional procedures, known as "enhancements," may be needed to maintain optimal vision.
